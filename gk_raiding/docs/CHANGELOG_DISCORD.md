# GK Raiding — Changelog (Unreleased)

### Pop Raiding

- Pop raiding now uses a tiered escalation system. Initial raids are small; spending Influence authorizes deeper captures, with each tier taking progressively more.
- Pops are taken proportionally across all eligible species rather than draining one group at a time.
- Occupied enemy planets are automatically raided each month. Multi-raider invasions split captures by army count.
- Reaping is now a policy option exclusive to Barbaric Despoiler civics and Hive Bodysnatcher.
- Hive Bodysnatcher now grants pop raiding access, the raiding bombardment stance, and Reaping.

### Escalation

- When a capture tier is filled, the war leader chooses: authorize deeper raids, hold position, switch to pillaging, demand tribute, or demand policy reforms.
- Influence costs scale with raid significance relative to empire size. With sufficient intel, the escalation report shows eligible pops remaining.
- Galaxy-wide opinion penalties for raided pops and escalation. Egalitarian and xenophile empires react more harshly.

### Civics and Policies

- Removed Robo-Liberators, Synthetic Liberation Corp, and Freedom Directive civics.
- Robots Only raiding policy is available to all vaguely robot related empire types.
- Emancipation has its own casus belli and requires Slavery to be banned (or Rogue Servitor).
- Nihilistic Acquisition no longer requires specific ethics.

### Pillaging

- Pillage yields reworked: base 2 loot-months, +3 from Pillage war goal, +1 from Despoliation tradition.
- Invaded and surrendered colonies receive a 5-year Ransacked modifier that blocks further pillaging.

### Privateer Enclaves

- Privateers now use the same upgrade rank system as mercenary enclaves.
- Patrons can convert between mercenary and privateer enclaves. Funded raids use fleet-value pricing.
- The raid initiator can cancel an active raid at no cost. AI empires now found privateer enclaves.
