Removed the pc_tidallylocked planet class and rebuilt its subclasses as a four-climate Tidal family, the same operation pc_hollow got. One class could only ever carry one city_color_lut, so Flaming, Eyeball and Meridiem all graded through a single lookup table despite being a scorched desert, a frozen sea and a mild archipelago; neutral was the least wrong compromise and suited none of them. On vanilla hosts each picks up a grade authored for its own look. The art decided the hosts: Flaming is orange rock and a dry canyon with no lava anywhere, so Desert; Eyeball's event picture is cracked pack ice and ice spires, so Arctic, which d_dust_desert had already guessed by excluding eyeball_world from its potential; Meridiem is turquoise shallows and green islands and its own text says a mild tropical climate prevails, so Tropical. The base class art, green plains and rivers under a warm-to-cool gradient, became a new normal-tier Twilight World on Continental rather than being discarded. Went with it: the habitability trait and its ten-class 60% spread, twelve terraform links, 28 deposit legality entries, a DEFAULT_HOME_PLANETS entry, the newfrontiers.14 capital package, the newfrontiers.69 default page entry, and six dangling Chinese loc keys that aliased the class name. Development of Midnight Lands and Development of Scorched Lands went with it.

The Tidal family scales its compensation by tier instead of sharing one signature. Karst gives every member the same district bonus and varies only tolerance, so it reads as a quality ladder; Tidal varies all three axes so each tier is a different shape of world. Energy climbs with hostility, 5% on the paradise, 15% on both normals, 25% on the extreme. Meridiem as paradise takes +25% tolerance and +5% energy with no penalty at all, because its description puts the habitable zone across the whole day side rather than the narrow belt the district penalty represents, and because a paradise carrying a real cost for a benefit worth nothing at 100% habitability would be strictly worse than the plain class. Twilight and Eyeball as normals take -10% districts and +15% energy. Flaming as extreme takes -25% tolerance, -10% districts and +25% energy, keeping the d_dust_desert volatile motes grant that was already legal on Desert. Flaming's tolerance was -0.15 and Meridiem's was +0.10, both off the -0.25 / +0.25 convention every other tier entry uses.

Cut Development of the Midnight Lands and Development of the Scorched Lands, the two decisions that came with the tidally locked class. Each traded habitability for room on a hostile hemisphere, +15% and +10% max districts respectively against -10% pop environment tolerance, seeding d_ore_rich_caverns or d_searing_desert on completion, with Scorched Lands twice as likely to hit its three-deposit branch on a Flaming world. Their two static modifiers, ten localisation keys across nine languages, and the is_tidal_subclass trigger written to regate them all went too. The district penalty stayed at -10% rather than the -25% a recoverable version could have carried, since with the decisions gone it is permanent.

Counts move to 5 on Continental, Tropical, Arctic and Desert, 4 elsewhere, 3 on Volcanic, for 51 subclass options. The Frontier column drops to Sterile alone. It cannot fold into the Dry column and be deleted, because the picker is hardcoded buttons and vanilla ships none for pc_sterile, so the .gui overwrite stays and Sterile moves up to the first row. GFX_evt_prairie_world was registered for the Twilight page: prairie_world.dds already shipped unused and its green plains, winding river and rock spires match the base tidal environment art closely, which avoided both drawing new art and repeating Eyeball's picture on two pages.

Renamed Infernal World to Magma World. Infernal named the heat but not the depth, which is the whole point of a Karst variant, and pc_volcanic is gated behind the Infernals Species Pack so the old name read as DLC branding. Magma carries both halves on its own, since molten rock below the crust is magma and only becomes lava once it surfaces. The modifier, scripted effect, planet modifier, popup option and localisation keys all moved; the art keeps its original names, so picture pc_infernal, infernal_planet_01_entity, GFX_evt_infernal_world and pm_infernal_world.dds are unchanged.

Sterile and Tidally-Locked stopped grading their surfaces as temperate. Both carried city_color_lut colorcorrection_continental.dds, inherited from the upstream template, which pushed greens across a lifeless world and a half-scorched eyeball. Both now use colorcorrection_neutral.dds, matching what vanilla gives every class whose art is not a standard biome. This is the only colour lever available: city_color_lut is a planet class property with no script effect behind it, so subclasses always grade as their base class. Planetary Diversity hits the same wall, since its dummy classes convert to vanilla bases at game start and their custom LUTs never render; the only PD grades that survive are on classes it keeps permanently.

Removed the pc_hollow planet class and rebuilt its subclasses as a four-climate Karst family. Hollow existed to support a survey-discovery chain (newfrontiers.4 flagged molten and frozen planets, then newfrontiers_action.1, .3 and .5 fired on_survey to change_pc them into pc_hollow with inherit_entity, revealing a habitable cave world inside an uninhabitable one) and dragged along a habitability trait, the unreachable Cave Dweller trait, two decisions with eight events, six expansion modifiers, two game rule clauses, a Frontier column slot and a DEFAULT_HOME_PLANETS entry. The discovery chain went with it. Karst World already carried the same cave idea on Arid, so Infernal moved to Volcanic and Subglacial to Alpine, both retiered to extreme with the Karst district bonus in place of their old output multipliers, and a new Hollow World joined Continental as a normal-tier variant filling the slot Primal vacated last pass. All four share planet_max_districts_mult 0.10 and grant d_karst_cave, checked legal on every host. The class art survives its class: pc_hollow's five environment files and hollow_world.dds now back the Continental subclass, so nothing new was drawn.

Mineral World moved from Alpine to Sterile to make room for Subglacial. Both are extreme, so neither class changed tier shape: Alpine keeps one extreme against three normals, Sterile returns to the three-extreme profile it had under Lava, Ash and Young. Mineral's flavour of mineral-saturated crust with no soil suits Sterile better regardless, and its d_crystalline_caverns grant is legal there. Subclass totals move 48 to 49 with every climate group level, cold and wet both 4/4/4 and the dry core matching.

Corrected two event pictures that were placeholders. The Sterile entry on the default page showed GFX_evt_archean_world, a subclass's art standing in for its base class, while GFX_evt_sterile_world was shipping unused; the Caustic page showed Ash art where vanilla's GFX_evt_toxic matches the Toxoids-derived surface. An audit of all fifteen triggered picture blocks found no other mismatches.

Climate-group membership was corrected alongside the moves, since three subclasses changed climate: Infernal and Mineral into is_dry_subclass, Subglacial into is_cold_subclass, and the new Hollow into is_wet_subclass rather than the is_dry_subclass it would have inherited from the Karst template it was copied from. Registering Hollow in is_planet_subclass matters most, since that trigger is what stops newfrontiers.4 and newfrontiers_action.14 re-rolling a planet that already has one. 429 orphaned localisation keys removed across all nine languages.

Forked Real Space - New Frontiers 3.3.2 into gk_newfrontiers so the suite is not bound to upstream's release schedule. The mod carries no Real Space dependency in its descriptor, but it did carry one in script: newfrontiers.1 (the subclass popup) and newfrontiers.4 (galaxy-wide subclass rolling), along with .5, .10 and .13, all ran from on_applying_rs_settings, an on_action fired by the Real Space settings menu and by nothing in New Frontiers itself. Standalone, none of them ever fired: no popup at game start and no subclasses anywhere in the galaxy. All five moved to on_game_start in their original relative order. The four subclasses_xN_setting density flags and the no_exotic_subclasses / no_legendary_subclasses toggles came from that same menu; with no menu they are simply never set, so density falls back to the base weight of 40 and nothing is stripped.

Removed all five New Frontiers origins: Twilight Ones, Awakened, Forsaken, Derelict Evolution and Sentient Planet. This took out common/governments/civics/newfrontiers_origins.txt, the whole of common/start_screen_messages/, interface/newfrontiers_origin_eventpictures.gfx with its six event pictures, the four origin-granted species traits (Opportunists, Survivalists, Evolved Machines, Extra Durable), the three Start Awakening decisions and Plant a Seed, events newfrontiers.12, .14 and .17, newfrontiers_action.6 through .12, newfrontiers_decision_event.36, the eleven Awakened and Forsaken solar system initializers, and the d_heart_of_the_overmind, d_seed_of_the_overmind, d_sarcophagus and d_protective_vault_system deposits. The overmind chain went whole rather than being de-gated because it hangs a destroy_country on losing the heart, selected by origin; stripping the origin check from that every_country block would have left a live landmine. 515 orphaned localisation keys removed across all nine languages. Only forgotten_world_system survives in the initializers file.

add_newfrontiers_starting_deposits no longer keys off origin. Every package in it sat inside one of four origin gates, so for any other empire the effect ran clear_deposits and then added nothing. That was unreachable upstream because the three classes it covers are all starting_planet = no, but it becomes a wiped capital the moment those classes are selectable. Packages are now keyed off the subclass alone. Sterile uses the old Forsaken organic-survivor package; the Derelict Evolution and Sentient Planet variants are dropped, as is Forsaken's Protective Vault System.

set_starting_subclass now branches on planet class rather than origin. Upstream's tidally-locked branch tested has_origin = origin_twilight_ones, and its sterile branch was an else carrying a limit on the three sterile origins. else ignores limit, so every class that fell past the earlier branches landed on a sterile subclass, pc_hollow included. Both are explicit is_planet_class checks now, and pc_hollow gained the infernal / subglacial branch it never had.

Cut the disaster suite: events/newfrontiers_disasters_events.txt in full, its on_action hooks on the five year pulse and the three building on_actions, the EVOLVED_STAR_PROJECT special project, and 1584 localisation keys. This also cleared the mod's second Real Space dependency, which the descriptor grep never showed because Real Space does not prefix its keys. stellar_evolution_effect switched stars into pc_k_giant_star, pc_g_giant_star, pc_m_super_star, pc_k_super_star, pc_a_super_star, pc_f_super_star and pc_b_super_star, none of which exist outside Real Space 4.0, so every branch of it threw a change_pc error. The disaster events also called set_gas_giant_small_rings, set_gas_giant_big_rings, set_small_planet_big_rings and set_small_planet_thin_rings, Real Space effects that build decorative ring ambient objects from Real Space models. Both are gone with the events that used them. Real Space star classes still appear as dead branches inside is_giant_star, planetary_catastrophe_is_possible and an old_star spawn_chance table, but each sits in an OR or additive list beside vanilla classes, so they evaluate false and log nothing. planetary_catastrophe_is_possible now has no callers.

Frontier-class capitals are stocked at game start instead of by the subclass popup. Vanilla's generate_start_deposits_and_blockers (01_start_of_game_effects.txt:786) opens with clear_deposits and then only re-adds deposits inside is_planet_class branches for eleven classes, none of which are pc_sterile, pc_tidallylocked or pc_hollow, so those capitals were wiped and left with nothing. Its blocker section is class-agnostic, which is why blockers appeared but deposits did not. The deposit pool was never the problem: pc_sterile and pc_tidallylocked each have 56 district-granting deposits whose potential admits them against pc_desert's 54. New colony_event newfrontiers.14 hooks empire_init_capital_colony, which runs after vanilla's game_start.12, and adds a per-class package on top without clearing. Packages are keyed to the base class rather than the subclass because empire init runs during galaxy generation, before on_game_start, so no subclass modifier exists yet. Every deposit was checked against its potential first; the Lava package could not be reused for Hollow because d_hot_springs, d_prosperous_mesa, d_rich_mountain and d_frozen_gas_lake are all illegal there. The same hook covers AI empires, since empire_init_capital_colony fires for every empire.

The subclass popup no longer regenerates the planet. All 48 add_*_starting_deposits calls were stripped from newfrontiers.18 to .68, another 39 from set_starting_subclass, and the 40 effect definitions deleted, taking about 1000 lines out of newfrontiers_starting_effects.txt. Choosing a subclass now only changes art and modifiers. Subclass-signature deposits such as Karst Cave, Grand Canyon and Serene Coastline no longer land on the capital, which is consistent with keeping subclass compensation in the static modifier, the only part that travels to wild-rolled planets.

The popup now opens with the plain version of your own world. Fourteen options were added above the subclass list, one per selectable base class, each triggered on the capital's class and named from its existing loc key in blue to match the normal tier the other options already use. Closing the event leaves you on that plain class, which is what the trailing Close option always did but never stated.

Added a fourth Frontier column to the empire creation homeworld picker, holding Hollow, Tidally-Locked and Sterile.
