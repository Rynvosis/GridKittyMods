Deposit eligibility keys on planet class rather than subclass. Cross-class subclass exclusions, gates of the form NOR has_modifier eyeball_world sitting inside another class's deposit, are the wrong axis now that deposits generate against the primary class. All 32 exclusion gates in the mod's own deposit files are gone, along with the 214 that vanished with the two deleted overwrites. The 62 inclusion gates stay: a bare or OR-nested has_modifier is a deposit's signature eligibility, and stripping those would have let d_salt_flat, d_ice_geyser and 13 others roll galaxy-wide. drop_weight biases stay too, since they shift odds within a class without splitting eligibility across classes, and d_karst_cave's now covers all four cavern subclasses rather than Karst alone. Two authoring bugs surfaced in the process: d_scorched_badlands had an empty OR where its class list should be, now is_dry matching its own drop_weight, and d_rich_lodes carried a stray empty NOT.

Extreme and exotic subclasses pay a real price and receive a real payout. The tier tag is a price tag, paradise buying +25% habitability, extreme charging -25% and doubling hazard and storm odds, so the contract is that extreme gives something back and paradise takes something. Fourteen subclasses were strictly dominant or strictly dominated inside their own tier. Aeolian, Effusive and Moraine paid the extreme habitability price for literally nothing. Rust took +15% society and +15% engineering research at no cost, Ancient +15% society, Karst and Hollow +10% districts. Panthalassic and Megaflora were pure penalties with no gain. Geothermal took +25% habitability, +15% energy and +4 generator districts for free, strictly dominating Oasis. Every extreme and exotic now runs to one formula: +2 districts and one rare deposit, then either +2 more districts or a second rare, plus a secondary production bonus matching its district field. Magma is the one exemption at +2 polytechnic, since that district type is scarcer and +2 carries a normal +4, and its secondary is research because the Thermotechnic Forum is a research district. Rust pays 25% building upkeep, Ancient 10% max districts, Karst, Hollow and Subglacial 15% build speed, Geothermal 20% building upkeep with its generator bonus cut to +2. Panthalassic gains 15% food, Megaflora 20%.

Exotic is a real tier rather than a label. is_exotic_subclass was defined with four members and referenced by nothing; it is now an OR member of is_extreme_subclass, so exotics inherit the doubled hazard weighting declaratively and the membership stays correct as the list changes. Dust joined Crystal and Noxious to form one exotic per strategic resource, keyed to climate: Crystal on cold tundra for rare crystals, Dust on dry arid for volatile motes, Noxious on wet tropical for exotic gases. Each is two modifiers, -25% habitability and +25% output from the job that carries its rare, so miners lift minerals and crystals together, technicians energy and motes, farmers food and gases. Each grants two deposits of its own rare. Noxious as the gas world works because it is tropical and keeps farmer jobs, which is what exotic gases ride. Dust left the empire creation picker, which along with Adiabatic moving from Desert to Arid brings both classes back to four options.

Sterile Worlds hook into the start-of-game rural district pipeline. Vanilla computes how many energy, mining and farming districts an empire deserves through 47 inline scripts adjusting three variables, then a placement step consumes them per planet class. With no farming districts, Sterile silently lost its entire agricultural allocation. The fix mirrors volcanic_world_setup: the farming allocation becomes Vault Complexes running the Hydroponics specialization, one district each, plus a hydroponics farm building, treating the Vault's 150 farmers as the farming district's 200. Energy and mining are left for vanilla to place normally, and no starting food stockpile is granted since the Vaults produce from turn one.

The hook is a dispatch rather than an inline edit, so the one frozen vanilla file stays a permanent one-line diff. rural_districts_placement.txt is copied verbatim with a single inline_script line added at the top calling gk_rural_districts_dispatch, which fans out to per-class setup scripts in the mod's own files. Each setup script consumes the variables it wants and clears them, so vanilla's branches below see only what is left, exactly how volcanic_world_setup opts out by clearing all three. Adding a future planet class means writing its setup script and one branch in the dispatch, never touching the vanilla copy again. This was chosen over redefining generate_start_buildings_and_districts, whose 101 lines are mostly inline_script calls to civics, origins and species that grow every DLC; freezing that list would silently stop new content adjusting district counts, whereas a stale placement file fails visibly as missing districts. Running before vanilla's Agriculture block also sidesteps its clamp of farming_districts to district_farming_max_add, which is zero on Sterile, so the vanilla copy needs no edit beyond the dispatch line.

Zeroing farming capacity through deposits was not enough on its own. With no food deposit a Sterile world reaches 0/0 farming districts, but the Agriculture Districts card still renders, because a district shows whenever its potential passes and Sterile remains on the standard district set. Hiding it requires district_farming.potential to be false, so common/districts/gk_newfrontiers_districts_OW.txt carries district_farming and district_orchard_forests copied verbatim from 02_rural_districts.txt and 05_wilderness_districts.txt with a single NOT is_planet_class pc_sterile added to each potential. Both are byte-identical to vanilla otherwise and the file sorts after both originals, so the later definition wins. This is the 268-line version of the overwrite rather than the 1,155-line district set, and the two districts stop receiving Paradox's future edits until re-copied. The deposit exclusion stays regardless, since a food deposit on Sterile would now feed a district that never appears.

Sterile Worlds lose agriculture and gain the Vault Complex, a district that stores resources and hosts five specializations. Farming district capacity is entirely deposit-driven: all 33 sources of district_farming_max_add in the mod are deposits, and the only non-deposit sources in vanilla are conditional planet modifiers like lush_planet and rich_mircoflora that never apply here. Only two of those 33 admitted pc_sterile, d_bubbling_swamp and d_fuming_bog, so deleting one line from each potential leaves a Sterile world with no farming capacity and district_farming simply never appears. No vanilla district is redefined and no district set is introduced. This is Planetary Diversity's approach as well: 191 planet classes, no common/districts directory at all, 148 of them on district_set standard, and food neutralised on domed colonies by replacement deposits granting district_farming_max_add 0. The alternatives were measured and rejected, since a real sterile_world district set would mean restating nine vanilla districts totalling about 1,155 lines plus an override of uses_district_industrial, and district_farming_max_mult -1.0 is a cap hack that leaves the district visible at zero.

district_vault is modelled on district_polytechnic, the closest vanilla analogue as a class-specific extra district with its own zone slot and deposit-driven capacity. It gates on is_planet_class rather than uses_district_set, since Sterile stays on the standard set. Each Vault gives 200 housing, 1000 country resource storage and 50 jobs through jobs/clerks_add, which resolves to clerks, production overseers for tankbound empires or logistics drones for gestalts. is_uncapped always no generates the district_vault_max_add and _mult modifiers, and capacity comes from eight new Sterile deposits weighted like the seven polytechnic deposits in 15_infernals_deposits.txt.

Five specializations sit in the new vault zone set, reached through slot_vault. Hydroponics and Garrison run vanilla's shared_food_zone and shared_fortress_zone at 150 jobs. Archive copies zone_research_unity's split scaled to 150, 30 across each of the three research jobs plus 60 unity. Stockpile gives 50 clerks, 100 traders and 2000 storage. Refinery is the sixth-turned-fifth, gas via a specialist job. 50 native jobs plus 150 from a zone lands on base_rural_district_jobs, a standard rural district's full complement, half native and half chosen. Gestalt and wilderness coverage is inherited free, since zone_clerks_add, zone_soldiers_add, zone_farmers_add, zone_trader_add, zone_researchers_add and zone_unity_jobs_add all branch internally.

Refinery Vault is written by hand because vanilla has no usable gas zone for a world without farming. zone_exotic_gases is not a gas zone at all: it lives in the food zone set, swaps to district_farming_exotic_gases, runs jobs/zone_farmers_add and converts farmers into extractors through planet_farmers_exotic_gases_produces_add, with tooltips that say as much. Refinery Vault instead employs gas_refiner, a genuine specialist job producing exotic gases, with gas_refiner_drone for gestalts, written as triggered_district_planet_modifier so it scales per district. The existing jobs/refiner_add script could not be reused because it is flat rather than district-scaled and additionally requires building_refinery. The zone unlocks off d_sterile_gas_pockets, which also grants vault capacity, so finding gas both widens the planet and opens the specialization.

Sterile Worlds get a full district-capacity deposit ecosystem, energy and mineral families as well as vault, so wild worlds and capitals both feel rich rather than empty. A wild Sterile world is high on both energy and minerals, matching a dry world's energy and a cold world's minerals at once, which suits its lifeless mineral-saturated identity. add_newfrontiers_starting_deposits no longer keys off origin. Every package in it sat inside one of four origin gates, so for any other empire the effect ran clear_deposits and then added nothing. That was unreachable upstream because the three classes it covers are all starting_planet = no, but it becomes a wiped capital the moment those classes are selectable. Packages are now keyed off the subclass alone. Sterile uses the old Forsaken organic-survivor package; the Derelict Evolution and Sentient Planet variants are dropped, as is Forsaken's Protective Vault System.

set_starting_subclass now branches on planet class rather than origin. Upstream's tidally-locked branch tested has_origin = origin_twilight_ones, and its sterile branch was an else carrying a limit on the three sterile origins. else ignores limit, so every class that fell past the earlier branches landed on a sterile subclass, pc_hollow included. Both are explicit is_planet_class checks now, and pc_hollow gained the infernal / subglacial branch it never had.

Cut the disaster suite: events/newfrontiers_disasters_events.txt in full, its on_action hooks on the five year pulse and the three building on_actions, the EVOLVED_STAR_PROJECT special project, and 1584 localisation keys. This also cleared the mod's second Real Space dependency, which the descriptor grep never showed because Real Space does not prefix its keys. stellar_evolution_effect switched stars into pc_k_giant_star, pc_g_giant_star, pc_m_super_star, pc_k_super_star, pc_a_super_star, pc_f_super_star and pc_b_super_star, none of which exist outside Real Space 4.0, so every branch of it threw a change_pc error. The disaster events also called set_gas_giant_small_rings, set_gas_giant_big_rings, set_small_planet_big_rings and set_small_planet_thin_rings, Real Space effects that build decorative ring ambient objects from Real Space models. Both are gone with the events that used them. Real Space star classes still appear as dead branches inside is_giant_star, planetary_catastrophe_is_possible and an old_star spawn_chance table, but each sits in an OR or additive list beside vanilla classes, so they evaluate false and log nothing. planetary_catastrophe_is_possible now has no callers.

Frontier-class capitals are stocked at game start instead of by the subclass popup. Vanilla's generate_start_deposits_and_blockers (01_start_of_game_effects.txt:786) opens with clear_deposits and then only re-adds deposits inside is_planet_class branches for eleven classes, none of which are pc_sterile, pc_tidallylocked or pc_hollow, so those capitals were wiped and left with nothing. Its blocker section is class-agnostic, which is why blockers appeared but deposits did not. The deposit pool was never the problem: pc_sterile and pc_tidallylocked each have 56 district-granting deposits whose potential admits them against pc_desert's 54. New colony_event newfrontiers.14 hooks empire_init_capital_colony, which runs after vanilla's game_start.12, and adds a per-class package on top without clearing. Packages are keyed to the base class rather than the subclass because empire init runs during galaxy generation, before on_game_start, so no subclass modifier exists yet. Every deposit was checked against its potential first; the Lava package could not be reused for Hollow because d_hot_springs, d_prosperous_mesa, d_rich_mountain and d_frozen_gas_lake are all illegal there. The same hook covers AI empires, since empire_init_capital_colony fires for every empire.

The subclass popup no longer regenerates the planet. All 48 add_*_starting_deposits calls were stripped from newfrontiers.18 to .68, another 39 from set_starting_subclass, and the 40 effect definitions deleted, taking about 1000 lines out of newfrontiers_starting_effects.txt. Choosing a subclass now only changes art and modifiers. Subclass-signature deposits such as Karst Cave, Grand Canyon and Serene Coastline no longer land on the capital, which is consistent with keeping subclass compensation in the static modifier, the only part that travels to wild-rolled planets.

The popup now opens with the plain version of your own world. Fourteen options were added above the subclass list, one per selectable base class, each triggered on the capital's class and named from its existing loc key in blue to match the normal tier the other options already use. Closing the event leaves you on that plain class, which is what the trailing Close option always did but never stated.

Cut every remaining feature suite that was not the planet subclass system: periodic weather and monster events (yearly ice ages, droughts, meteorite showers, Wild Hunt, Abyssal Devourers, resource depletion, the pc_nuked decay chain), colony growth events including the ethos enclave and criminal faction pools, the Long Night darkness invasion with its night_monsters armies and Dead World hardened warriors, the Ancient Machine gas giant quest chain, the Ancient Relics mysterious monolith dig site and its relic, and the displaced-pop pioneer/smuggler/drone jobs. Twelve whole files went, along with eight now-empty common/ directories. The Forgotten World system survives, minus its create_archaeological_site call.

Finished the disaster cull the events file alone did not complete: thirteen decisions (planetary evacuation and its lithoid variant, obligatory transference, refugee reception center, receiving terminal, bastion project, the hot and cold shelter projects, disaster solutions, and five abolish counterparts), events newfrontiers_decision_event.1 and .17 to .24, newfrontiers.6, building_orbit_stabilizer, building_planetary_core_regulator (orphaned by the Awakened origin), and the three permanently-unresearchable disaster techs. Fifty-two static modifiers removed after a reference scan, of which plasmoid_life_forms, energy_grid, scarlet_flashes and the three depreservation_process entries were already dead upstream, defined but never applied. Eleven orphaned weather and monster scripted effects went with them.

Events newfrontiers_decision_event.25 to .33 were restored after being cut with the disaster range: they belong to Cave Network Expansion, Ice Caves Formation and Planetary Excavation, all subclass decisions. Kept alongside them are decision_protective_equipment, which gates on lava_world and ash_world, the cloud platform pair for Noxious World, the nature preserve abolition, and the seven blocker-clearing techs. building_pharmaceutical_production_complex stays; it gates on the Algae World deposit.

Around 5,000 orphaned localisation keys removed across all nine languages. The mod is now 9,002 lines of events and 23,928 lines of common across sixteen directories.

Added a fourth Frontier column to the empire creation homeworld picker, holding Hollow, Tidally-Locked and Sterile. That picker is entirely hardcoded: each planet is a buttonType named for its class at hand-placed coordinates, with three backing frames and three headers. Rather than overwrite interface/customize_species_editors.gui, the window is redefined in interface/gk_newfrontiers_species_editors.gui, which is how Planetary Diversity does it; a same-named window in a later-loading file replaces the original. Vanilla parks TYPE_HEADER_04 ("climate_volcanic") and the pc_volcanic button on top of the Cold column at the same coordinates, so the new column uses its own header id and a new climate_gk_frontier key rather than adopting that half-finished one. The @ variables the window depends on live in the vanilla file's header and do not carry across, so they are redeclared. Anything else redefining planet_class_editor still collides, Planetary Diversity included.

The three classes are selectable to back that column. All three carried starting_planet = no, and pc_sterile and pc_hollow had no climate at all, which upstream never needed because the four origins were the only way to hold one. Sterile groups as dry (Lava, Ash, Archean and Young are all hot) and Hollow as cold; the column header is a label and does not have to agree with either. Enabled trait_pc_hollow_preference, which upstream left commented out with its icon and TRAIT_HOLLOW_PREFERENCE_EFFECT tooltip already shipping, so a Hollow start would otherwise have had no habitability preference trait at all.

Planet textures, planet-view environments and event pictures are tracked through git-LFS via .gitattributes scoped to each of those three directories, matching the gk_traits arrangement. The 3.9 MB of interface icons stay in normal git. 1164 files in LFS, 7.2 MB of script and localisation outside it.
